Key research highlights

1. Hadley circulation exhibits prominent variability from region-to-region

Most of the studies till date have concentrated on studying the variability and trends in the strength and extent of the global mean Hadley Circulation (HC), which may not often reflect the region-to-region differences in the HC. There are several unanswered questions pertaining to the characteristics of the various regional HCs. In this study we provide a comprehensive analysis of the regional HCs across six distinct geographic domains.

Reference: Pratiksha & Neena (2023), AGU Fall Meeting Abstract

2. Tropical eddies play a crucial role in modulating the strength of regional Hadley circulation

In this work, we explore how deep convection triggered by low-level convergence associated with Mixed Rossby-Gravity waves plays a key role in modulating the strength of the East Pacific Hadley circulation.

Reference: Pratiksha et al. 2025, JGR: Atmospheres https://doi.org/10.1029/2024JD042980

3. Influence of extratropical circulation on strength and extent of regional Hadley circulations

The occurrence of westerly wind ducts over the eastern Pacific and Atlantic during boreal winter enables potential vorticity intrusions from the extratropics into the tropics. We aim to investigate how these extratropical intrusions influence the mass flux, as well as the strength and extent of regional Hadley circulations.
